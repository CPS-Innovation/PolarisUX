Case summary

Provide a brief overview of the charges and facts in a case and the current bail or remand status of the suspect(s). You should also outline your rationale for applying the Threshold Test.

Notes for completing this section

You must be confident that all 5 conditions of the threshold test have been met. These are:

  1. there are reasonable ground to suspect that the person to be charged has committed the offence
  2. further evidence can be obtained to provide a realistic prospect of conviction
  3. the seriousness or the circumstances of the case justifies the making of an immediate charging decision
  4. there are continuing substantial grounds to object to bail in accordance with the Bail Act 1976 and in all the circumstances of the case it is proper to do so
  5. it is in the public interest to charge the suspect

Where any of the conditions are not met, the Threshold Test cannot be applied and the suspect cannot be charged.

Evidential analysis

Provide your analysis of whether or not, and in what ways, the evidence proves all elements of the offence or offences in accordance with the Threshold Test. Your assessment should anticipate any likely issues for trial and suggest ways to overcome potential challenges to the prosecution case.

Notes for completing this section

Ensure you have considered:

  • whether the evidence is reliable, credible and admissible
  • any evidence that could be used by the defence to undermine the case and what can be done to counter that
  • the strength of the evidence if a victim is unwilling to testify or withdraws their support
  • if any alternative lines of enquiry should be explored or additional evidence sought that would strengthen the case
  • whether any further evidence will become available and how to deal with this - and what to do if it does not become available within a clear time frame

Reference any points of law or CPS policy in your assessment that may be relevant.

Public interest assessment

Based on your analysis of the case, explain if and how a prosecution is required in the public interest, referencing relevant parts of the code to support your rationale.

Help with public interest assessment

Your assessment should consider and balance:

  • the seriousness of the offence
  • the level of culpability of the suspect
  • the circumstances of and the harm caused to the victim
  • the suspect’s age and maturity at the time of the offence and whether this is a mitigating factor
  • the impact on the victim or the wider community
  • whether the prosecution is a proportionate response
  • whether sources of information require protecting

You should distinguish between factors that may be considered a mitigating feature and those which, taken together or alone, outweigh the need for a prosecution

Where appropriate, you should consider whether an out-of-court disposal is a suitable response to the offender and the seriousness of the crime.

View detailed guidance on assessing public interest in the case strategy hub (opens in new tab)

Witness and victim strategy

Where possible at this stage of the case, highlight key witness and victim information and actions required so that they can support the case.

Help with witness and victim strategy

Your considerations of witness and victim strategy should include:

  • identifying the crucial witness or witnesses in the case
  • whether a Standard or Enhanced level of service is needed
  • if there are any likely attendance issues or a risk of withdrawal. You should consider how to manage this, particularly for evidence-led prosecutions in domestic abuse (DA) cases.
  • any special measures required for vulnerable or intimidated witnesses
  • how intermediaries or the use of an interpreter may help and which language they must be able to speak

Trial strategy

Outline the trial strategy you’ve identified to best support a successful prosecution.

Help with trial strategy

Depending on the progression of the case, you should consider.

Trial practicalities:

  • s.10 admissions to be sought
  • witnesses to be agreed (s.9) and called
  • media exhibits, such as CCTV, body-worn vide or 999 call recordings, that should be presented in court
  • Res gestae or other issues with admissibility of evidence
  • applications that my be made, such as Hearsay or Bad character

You should also specify any sentencing issues or ancillary orders such as:

  • whether protective orders are required
  • forfeiture and destructive order applications (for indecent images, drugs and paraphernalia, weapons.)
  • reporting restrictions
  • whether Victim Personal Statements or Community Impact Statements would be of benefit

Defence engagement such as:

  • whether there been any defence pre-charge engagement
  • any requests for early disclosure
  • any defence indications on allocation or offers of pleas

Allocation

With reference to the allocation guidelines and offence specific sentencing guidelines, make your recommendation for how the case should be allocated.

Help with allocation

You should consider whether:

  • the offence is summary only or indictable, with awareness that mandatory sentences apply for indictable-only offences
  • it is an either way offence
  • there are aggravating or mitigating factors in the case
  • the case is suitable for summary trial

View guidance on allocation, sending and committal for sentence on CPS SharePoint (opens in new tab)

View current sentencing guidelines on the Sentencing Council website (opens in new tab)

Disclosure

Consider appropriate actions that can be take around disclosure at this stage and pre-empt disclosure issues that may be important as the case progresses.

Help with disclosure

Where possible, consider:

  • whether any early common law disclosure is required
  • whether all relevant evidence identified by the police is likely to be disclosable material
  • police compliance with requirement for completed MG3 insert on RASSO and CCU cases
  • whether a Disclosure Management Document is required
  • whether police have complied with the requirement to supply an Information Management Document (IMD) or equivalent, outlining information about reasonable lines of enquiry

Advocate and op.delivery instructions

Provide a summary of notes and actions for a prosecuting advocate and operational delivery staff.

Help with advocate and operational delivery notes

Depending on the progression of the case, you should provide instruction on:

The remand or bail status of the suspect including

  • details of the remand application and the grounds
  • confirmation that the MG7 is adequate
  • whether BAA is appropriate in this case
  • suitable bail conditions, where bail is considered appropriate

Matter for the advocate to address in court, for example

  • acceptable pleas
  • protective orders required, such as a restraining order
  • is compensation required
  • sentencing uplift requests for hate crimes or assaults on emergency workers

You should also include any relevant actions for operational delivery staff administering the case.
