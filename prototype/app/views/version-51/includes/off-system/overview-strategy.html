Prosecution Strategy

Prosecution Strategy

This section should be completed by the lawyer and include the following.

  • Case/Investigation objectives
  • Origin of the investigation
  • Agreed case/investigation parameters
  • Trial strategy including any decision to draft multiple indictments or joinder applications
  • Redaction strategy and DPA considerations

Pre-charge, the prosecutor should set out how the CPS has been working with the investigation team and outline any relevant decisions or agreements which have been taken pre charge.

The prosecutor should consider:

  • What issues need to be resolved pre charge?
  • What is the strategy for overcoming those issues?
  • What are the likely charges under consideration? This should list viable charges and questionable offences with critical analysis.
  • What evidence will be required to prove these and how will that be presented?
  • How will the prosecutor keep the investigation focused?
  • What timescales have been agreed and why?
  • Any suspects not proceeded with?
  • Have the charges been selected based on any trial management considerations?
  • Any VRR or VLC considerations
  • Redaction strategy

Post charge, the prosecutor should consider the following points:

  • What are the issues for trial and how will they be addressed?
  • Any outstanding actions which need to be resolved, additional evidence or disclosure?
  • If there is to be more than one trial how this will be managed?
  • Which witnesses need to be called and why?

Any changes in strategy should be recorded as a new entry, dated appropriately. Prosecutors should consider the need to record updated strategy following key events e.g. ECPC, charging, PTPH and stage dates.

Disclosure Strategy

Disclosure Strategy

This section should be completed by the lawyer.

This section should set out the key areas considered for disclosure purposes and how the prosecutor intends to deal with them. It will include the following considerations:

  • When did the investigation commence for the purposes of disclosure?
  • What defences can reasonably be anticipated? Were any disclosed in interview?

Prosecutors should record here strategy agreed with UKLE on pursuing reasonable lines of enquiry.

Prosecutors should also record here the strategy agreed with UKLE and other relevant parties on the approach to be taken to categories of relevant material. This will include, but not be limited to, digital devices and electronic material, third party material, linked investigations, telecoms data, forensic material, product arising from covert tactics (including application of applicable MOUs), visual and audio footage and international material.

In cases involving multiple devices, at an early stage the prosecutor should agree any approach to review with the case team. This includes liaison pre-arrest/search to agree parameters of the search and strategy for securing relevant material. This section will address this approach for both evidential and disclosure purposes. It will also address any issues around consent and presentation of material.

Lawyers must complete the Disclosure Record Sheet on CMS (Modern) in addition. Where the lawyer chooses to record strategy on the DRS, they should cross reference the relevant entry in this section.
