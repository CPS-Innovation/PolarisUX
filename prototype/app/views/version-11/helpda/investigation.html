Charging decision

Court hearing

The void

This is where the Crown Prosecution Service review the evidence and materials submitted by the police to ascertain whether to charge the case. The Prosecuting lawyer looking at the case follows guidance set out by the Director of Public Prosecutions for England and Wales

The prosecutor is looking for the case to satisfy two key parts of this guidance known as the Full Code Test which are:

  • There is enough probable evidence to secure a conviction if the case went to Court
  • Making a charge is in the public interest, for example the offender is likely to reoffend

Charging the case will mean your case will proceed to the next step. You may at this point hear that the case is not proceeding, known as no further action.

If either the police or CPS later decide not to proceed or substantially change the charge(s) they have previously discussed with you, you will be told the reasons why within five working days.

What signifies this stage?

When the police submit a case to CPS they disclose all materials pertaining to the case. This means they may have asked you for a lot of details upfront. This can feel intimidating but is crucial in getting the materials to CPS to charge. At this point:

  • Police have take a statement
  • You have been asked to provide evidence to form a case, including your phone, photos of injuries
  • The defendant has had an interview with the police

You may have further requests from the police for more information in this time. This isn’t [because they don’t believe you]. It is likely that the prosecution team at CPS have asked the police to retrieve more information to satisfy the points above.
